>
> **Document type:** `guide` | **Diátaxis:** Tutorial
>
> A Guide (tutorial) walks a reader through a complete workflow from start to
> finish — not just steps, but enough context to understand *what is happening
> and why* at each stage. The goal is learning, not just task completion. By the
> end, the reader should be able to operate independently in the area the guide
> covers.
>
> Guides are rare (~5% of content). Use one when:
> - A person is new to a platform, tool, or domain and needs an integrated view
> - The topic requires combining multiple systems or procedures in a specific order
> - Existing patterns and references exist but have no onboarding-level entry point
>
> Guides reference procedures (patterns, runbooks) and concepts (overviews) but
> are not a substitute for them. Keep rationale light — link to the concept
> document instead of embedding explanation. Keep steps concrete enough to follow
> but focused on learning, not production execution.
>
> **Do not use** for reference lookup, operational procedures, or design-time
> architecture patterns. If a reader just needs to *do* something, point them to
> a pattern or runbook instead.
